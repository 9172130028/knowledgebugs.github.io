1) Chemical Reactions and Equations

  • A Physical change can be easily reversed but a chemical change cannot be reversed easily.

  • example of physical change are:evaporation,melting of wax,freezing of water.

  • Example of chemical changes are :conversion of milk to curd,rusting of iron,digestion of food.

    Chemical Reactions

  • A chemical reaction is a charge in which one or more substances reactants reacts to form new substances with entirely different properties.

  • The reacting species (molecule,atom,ion) are known as reactants (the substances that undergo chemical change in the chemical reaction)

  • The new species formed as a result of the reaction are called products. (the new substances formed during reaction)
    eg:

    • h + o2 --> h2o
    (hydrogen) (oxygen) (water)

    In the above chemical equation, water is end product and hydrogen and oxygen are the reactants.

    Identification of chemical reaction

    A chemical reaction can be identified by

  • Change in state
  • Change in colour
  • Evolution of Gas
  • Changein Tempreature
  • Formation of Precipitate

    Chemical Equations

  • A Chemical equationis the symbolic representation of chemical reaction.
    eg:

    • Reaction of burning of methane gas can be written in words as :

    Methane+oxygen --> dioxide+Water
    (Reactants) (Products)

    This equation is called word equation.

  • The word equation can be changed into a chemical equation bye writing symbols and formulae of the substance in place of their name.

    Writing a chemical equation:

  • A chemical equation shows a change of reactants to products through an arrow(->) placed between them.
  • On the left hand side (LHS) of the arrow,reactants are writen with a plus sign(+).
  • On the right hand side (RHS) products are also writen with a plus sign.
  • The arrow head points towards the products and shows the direction of the reaction.
    Example: Mg+O2 --> Mg0
    (Reactants) (Product)

    • In above equation, number of magnesium and oxygen atoms are not same on both side of an equation. Such an unbalanced equation is called Skeletal Chemical Equation.

    Balanced Chemical Equations:

  • A Balanced chemical is that in which the total numberof atoms of each element are equal on both sides of the equation.
  • The balancing of a chemical equation is based on law of conservation of mass.
  • According to law of conservation of mass,mass can neither be created nor be destroyed during a chemical reaction.
  • The method used for balancing chemical equations is called hit and trial method.
  • In this method,the number of atoms of each element remains the same, befor and after a chemical reaction.

    • Balancing of a chemical Equation:

  • Many steps are involved in balancing a chemical Equation:
  • The steps are:

    • A) Writing unbalanced equation and enclosing the formulae in brackets.
    (Na) + h2o --> (NAOH) + (H2)
    Sodium Water Sodium hydroxide hydrogen

    B) Making list of number of atoms of different elements as present in unbalanced equation.

    Element No. of atoms in reactants (LHS) No. of Atoms in products (RHS)
    Na 1 1
    H 2 3
    O 1 1

    C) Balancing first element from the table shown above, it is clear that it is only the hydrogen atoms,which are unbalanced . So , firstly we try to balance it .

    Atoms In Reactants In Products
    Initially 2 (in H20) 3 (1 in NaOH and
    2 in H2)
    To balance 2x2 2x1 in NaOH
    2 in H2=4 H atoms

    D) Balancing Second element We examine the obtained equation and select another element which is still unbalanced . In the above equation , Na is still unbalanced . To balance the number of Na-atoms .

    Atoms of Na In Reactants In Products
    Initially 1 (in Na) 2 (in NaOH)
    To balance 2x1 2

    Thus,after balancing Na and removing the brackets, we get the equation:

    2Na + 2 H2O ----> 2 NaOH + H2

    E) Balancing other elements If we further examine the reaction, no element is found to be unbalanced. This method of balancing chemical Equation is called hit-and-trial method.

    Types of chemical Reactions

    1] Combination Reaction

    A reaction in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product,is called Combination reaction

    Some Examples are:

    i] Calcium oxide (quick line) reacts vigarously with water to form calcium hydroxide (slaked lime)
    The reaction is highly exothermic,as a lot of heat is produced during the reaction

    Calcium oxide(quick lime)+ Water---->Calcium hydroxide(slaked lime) +Heat

  • Solution of calcium hydroxide(slaked lime) is used for white washing walls
  • Calcium hydroxide reacts slowly with carbon dioxide in air to form a thin layer of calcium carbonate,on the walls which gives a shiny appearance to the walls.
  • Calcium carbonate is formed after two to three days of white washing

    • Calcium hydroxide(aq) + Carbon dioxide --->Calcium carbonate(s)+Water(l)

    ii] Burning of Coal

    Carbon + Oxygen ---> Carbon dioxide

    iii] Reaction betweenhydrogen gas and oxygengas to form water.

    Hydrogen + Oxygen ---> Water

    2] Decomposition Reaction

    A Reaction in which a single reactant breaks down to form two or more products,is known as Decomposition Reaction

    This reaction is opposite to Combination Reaction

    On the basis of the form of energy required for the reaction , these reactions are of three Types:

    i]Thermal Decomposition

    These reactions use the energy in the form of heat for Decomposition of the reactant.

    e.g: Calcium carbonate on heating , decomposes to give calcium oxide and carbon dioxide. Calcium oxide is used for manufacturing of cement.

    Heat

    Calcium Carbonate(limestone) ------> Calcium oxide (Quick lime) +Carbon Dioxide

    ii] Electrolysis

    These reactions involve the use of electrical energy for the Decomposition of the reactant molecules.

    e.g: when electric current is passed through water, it decomposes to give oxygen and hydrogen.

    electric current

    water(l)------->hydrogen +oxygen

    iii] Photolsis or photochemical Decomposition

    These reactions involve the use of light energy for the purpose of decomposition.

    e.g: When silver chloride is exposed to sun light,it decomposes to give silver metal and chlorine gas.

    sunlight

    silver chloride(white)------> silver(greyish white)+ chlorine(yellowish green)

    3] Displacement Reaction

    When a more reactive element displaces less reactive element from it's Compounds, it is called Displacement Reaction

    This reaction is of 2 Types:

    i] Single Displacement reaction

    It is a type of chemical reaction where an element reacts with a Compounds and takes the place of another element in that Compound is calledSingle Displacement

    e.g: Zinc being more reactive than Cu, displacesCu from CuSO4 solution and form new product,zinc Sulphate and Cu metal.

    zinc metal+copper sulphate solution(blue)------>zinc sulphate solution(colourless) +copper metal(brown)

    ii] Double Displacement Reaction:

    The reaction in which two different ions or group of atoms in the reactant molecules are displaced bye each other is called double Displacement reaction

  • is also called precipitation reaction as precipitate is produced in such reactions

    e.g: On adding sodium sulphate to barium chloride, a curdy white precipitation of barium sulphate and a solution of sodium chloride are formed.

    sodium sulphate+barium chloride---->barium sulphate(white ppt.)+sodium chloride

  • The reaction in which acid or acidic oxide react with base or basic oxide to form salt and water are called neutrilization reactions.

    (sodium hydroxide)+(sulphuric acid)--->(sodium sulphate)+(water)
